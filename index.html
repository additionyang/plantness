Obsubject Plants : Sense

Lean in to sonified signals

Conscious beings are seen as subjects; whereas the unspeakable natures are objects. Our world has been invaded by
cyborgs: machine learning, artificial intelligence, robot with sensor, whale wearing radar instrument…...such rise
of quasi-objects cast doubts on the modern segregation between subject and object. How human treats non-human
becomes an issue of significance.

Plants possess the trait “in-between objects and living things,” but they’re not the low-end version of animals. Every
plant has its character, just like their distinctive growths and intangible reactions to the outer world. We attempt to
translate and deliver such messages through design.

Plants can not only perceive the outside world, but also respond to the outside stimuli to varying degrees. Through
two tiny electrodes, we amplify and translate the signal of the plant's response to the outer world, so that people can
hear the sound of plant.

Obsubject Plants : Will

Designed to be subverted

Botanist Hope Jahren found out that under identical conditions, plants of the same species have different ways of growing.
Apart from genes, it’s their own will.

The half sphere bottoms allow the containers to vary with the direction of the plants' growths, genuinely reflecting their
changes through the reflection on the metal surface.Eventually, people will see an artificial system progress from stable
to chaotic, which is the choice of natural randomness.
